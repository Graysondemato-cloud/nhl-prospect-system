What the model has learned
Skating: most reliable skillAcceleration + edgework translate at 2.4x the rate of static skills. McDavid (10), MacKinnon (10), Ovechkin (9) — all elite skaters as prospects.
Playmaking vision: best predictor of stardomPlaymakers outperform pure scorers in long-term NHL value. Crosby (10 vision), Quinn Hughes (10 vision). Vision is hardest to teach — high marks here are extremely valuable.
Compete / motor: most underratedVictor Eklund — 5'11", 161 lbs, rated elite in motor + forecheck by every scout. Comped to Konecny (5-10/175) and Jarvis (5-10/179). Compete + skating + IQ beat size every time.
Size: a comp factor, not a penaltySize is used to match comparables only — not as a score penalty. 6'5" player with 4/10 skating is penalized. 5'10" player with 9/10 compete is not. Size without mobility = historic bust signal.
Decision speed: the AHL trapThe most common reason prospects stall. Time and space compress dramatically at NHL level. Hardest skill to develop — low marks are a genuine ceiling concern.
